Hypothesis 1: Eye Diversity Throughout The World

Logged: 2025-09-21

It's fun to think about where the first humans originated from, on the surface level at least. The deeper you get, the more confusing things can be, and the more thoughts that pop into your head. Now, I'm no scientist, nor do I have a college degree, but the history of the world, and the people especially, really does fascinate me. So last night while looking into the DAI people, along with Taoism and Daoism, I started to think about why Asian people would have different eye formations than that of the majority of the rest of the world. My hypothesis: the environment played a big role in shaping people's features, and that the Epicanthic eye fold is an evolutionary change that helped early humans protect their eyes from specific things in their surroundings, like bright sunlight reflecting off of snow or sand.
